Rezdiffra (resmetirom): Unlocking system-level value

A synthetic liver allocation model showing how earlier MASH treatment may reduce transplant demand, ease waitlist pressure, and create value beyond treated patients.

Decision problemCan earlier treatment change downstream transplant-system demand and outcomes?
Strategic insightEarlier MASH treatment may create spillover value across the transplant system by reducing demand, easing waitlist pressure, and improving organ allocation efficiency.

Precision Screening Case Study

Targeting AF Screening for Higher-Value Care

Microsimulation model to guide value-based implementation of atrial fibrillation (AF) screening by identifying which populations, screening pathways, and risk thresholds generate the strongest clinical and economic value.

Decision problem Does incorporating individualized AF risk into screening eligibility and utilizing wearable monitoring create more clinical and economic value?
Strategic insight Risk-informed eligibility and wearable monitoring can support earlier detection, reduce complications, and improve implementation efficiency.

Value Evidence Case Study

Turning Wegovy (Semaglutide) into High-Value Care for MASH

A microsimulation model evaluating the cost-effectiveness of Wegovy (semaglutide 2.4 mg) for MASH under different pricing scenarios.

Decision problem Does semaglutide deliver strong clinical and economic value for patients with moderate-to-advanced fibrosis due to MASH?
Strategic insight Semaglutide’s value depends on fibrosis stage and price. At negotiated pricing, it becomes a high-value option for MASH, with stronger economic value in more advanced fibrosis.

Turning Medicaid Claims into Actionable Evidence for Pediatric Mental Health Care

A real-world evidence study using Medicaid claims to uncover how pediatric mental health patients navigate care, where service gaps emerge, and how payers and health systems can better target resources.

Decision problem How do pediatric Medicaid patients with mental health conditions use healthcare services, and where do care gaps emerge?
